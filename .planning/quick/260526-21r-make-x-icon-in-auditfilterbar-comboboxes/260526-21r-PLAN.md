    WHY pointerdown matters (do not skip): Radix Popover's `PopoverTrigger` uses `onPointerDown` to open the popover, not `onClick`. If you handle `onClick` only, the popover opens BEFORE your click handler runs, and the user sees a flash-open-then-close (or just open) instead of a clean dismiss. `e.preventDefault()` on pointerdown also blocks the trigger's focus grab so the popover state stays clean for the next pointerdown directly on the trigger.

    WHY a span with `role="button"` instead of a real `<button>`: the parent `PopoverTrigger asChild` clones its single child (the outline Button) and forwards refs/events. Nesting a `<button>` inside a `<button>` is invalid HTML — browsers will sometimes split the DOM, sometimes silently strip the inner button — and React will log a hydration warning. A span with `role="button"` + `tabIndex={0}` + the keyboard handler is the accessible escape hatch.
